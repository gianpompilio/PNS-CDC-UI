COVID-19 Daily Check-In

Why you need to register

You’re legally required to self-isolate if you have tested positive for COVID-19 or have been told by Public Health that you may have been exposed and need to self-isolate. If you tested positive for COVID-19 and are symptomatic, you must self-isolate for 10 days from symptom onset. If you tested positive for COVID-19 and are asymptomatic, you must self-isolate for 10 days from your positive COVID-19 test. If you were identified by Public Health as a close contact, you must self-isolate for 14 days from the most recent exposure.

During your self-isolation, you must register for COVID-19 Daily Check-In and check-in every day until the end of your isolation period.

How to register

You will need the following to complete your registration:

  • Health Card Number
  • iolation end date (as directed by Public Health)
  • email address

What to do after you register

After you register:

  • you will receive email for registration confirmation and daily check-in reminders
  • you must check in every day until the end of your isolation
  • you must continue to self-isolate

Learn more: how to self-isolate.

Privacy Notice

When you use the COVID-19 Daily Check In service your personal information is collected under the authority sections 8, 15 and 50 of the Health Protection Act (PDF).

The personal information collected will be:

  • only used to facilitate the COVID-19 Daily Check-In process, unless otherwise authorized by law or your express consent
  • subject to the Government of Nova Scotia’s security and privacy policies
  • limited to aggregate, de-identified information for any secondary use without express consent

To read more about how government respects your health privacy when interacting with us, visit the Personal Health Information Act website. For questions about how your personal information and health information is handled in relation to this service and the Personal Health Information Act (PHIA), you may contact the Privacy and Access Office at phia@novascotia.ca.
